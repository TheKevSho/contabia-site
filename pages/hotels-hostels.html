OTA commission intelligence

Three OTAs. Three commission models. Most bookkeepers get at least one wrong.

Each OTA handles commissions differently. If your books don't account for all three correctly, your P&L is distorted, your channel profitability is wrong, and you're making decisions on bad data. Your agent handles all three automatically.

Booking.com — Monthly Invoice Model

Booking.com BV · Netherlands · Foreign entity → documento soporte required
Guest pays you directly → Booking invoices you monthly → You pay the invoice later

The problem: Most hostels expense the commission when they pay the invoice — not when the stay happens. This overstates profit in the stay month and understates it in the payment month. The agent accrues in the stay month, reclassifies when the invoice arrives, and clears when paid.

Bank visibility: Visible — you see the payment out.

Hostelworld — Phantom Commission Model

Hostelworld · Ireland · Foreign entity → documento soporte required
Guest pays deposit to HW → HW keeps their commission → Guest pays balance at checkout

The problem: The commission never flows through your bank. You only collect the checkout balance. If you don't gross up, your revenue is understated by every Hostelworld commission. ADR looks lower. RevPAR is wrong. Booking.com looks "expensive" (visible invoice) while Hostelworld looks "free" (no payment out). The error is invisible in bank reconciliation.

Bank visibility: INVISIBLE — no transaction to match. This is why it gets missed.

Airbnb — Pass-Through Model

Airbnb · Ireland · Foreign entity → documento soporte required
Airbnb collects full amount → Deducts service fee → Sends you the net payout

The problem: Same gross-up requirement as Hostelworld. You receive the net amount, but your books need to show gross revenue minus commission. Without the gross-up, you understate revenue and can't accurately compare channel profitability.

Bank visibility: Partially visible — you see the net deposit but not the gross or the fee.

All three are foreign entities. Every commission from every OTA requires a documento soporte for tax deductibility per DIAN Concepto 192/2025. Without it, you're paying the commission AND losing the tax deduction. Your agent generates these automatically.

Tax recovery

Your OTA commissions are probably not deductible right now.

Per DIAN Concepto 192/2025, bank statements and platform payment receipts are NOT valid fiscal support for foreign purchases. Every commission to Booking.com (Netherlands), Hostelworld (Ireland), and Airbnb (Ireland) requires a documento soporte to be tax-deductible.

Most hostels don't generate them. That means you're paying 15-18% commission to Booking.com AND losing the 35% corporate tax deduction on that expense AND missing the IVA credit on OTA commissions.

Your agent generates documento soporte for every foreign purchase automatically — OTAs, software subscriptions (LobbyPMS, Google Workspace, Canva), digital advertising (Meta, Google Ads), Amazon purchases. Every one of these becomes a deductible expense with IVA credit eligibility.

Typical impact: A hostel with COP 47-132M/year in foreign purchases that doesn't generate documentos soporte loses COP 16-46M/year in deductions plus COP 6-11.4M/year in unclaimed IVA credits on OTA commissions alone. The ContabIA service costs COP 6M/year.
