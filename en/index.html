Agent intelligence

What the agent does every close

Not one function. The entire bookkeeping workload — reconciliation, taxes, payroll, anomalies — executed with machine precision, every month.

OTA reconciliation: Three different commission models (Booking.com net, Expedia gross, Hostelworld/Airbnb gross with separate invoicing) — correctly reconciled against bank deposits.

Complete tax engine: IVA, withholding tax (both directions, new 10 UVT threshold), ICA, GMF, autorretención, and documento soporte for ALL foreign purchases.

Per-employee liabilities: Prestaciones sociales, cesantías, prima, vacaciones — tracked per person, not as flat percentages. Accurate to the individual.

Multi-currency: Same-day TRM conversion for all USD/EUR transactions. OTAs pay in foreign currency — the agent handles conversion and reconciliation.

Dual-format reporting: PUC for Colombian legal compliance and USALI for international hotel operations standards. Two formats from the same data, automatic.

Anomaly detection: Duplicate charges, vendor costs creeping up silently, deposit discrepancies — the agent doesn't get tired and doesn't forget on month 7.

Every Colombian business

Your foreign purchases aren't deductible. Unless you have the documento soporte.

Per DIAN Concepto 192/2025, bank statements and platform receipts are NOT valid fiscal support. Every payment to a foreign vendor — OTA commissions, software subscriptions, cloud hosting, digital ads, e-commerce — requires a documento soporte to be tax-deductible. Most businesses don't generate them. Your agent does, automatically.

At the 35% corporate tax rate, every COP 1M in foreign expenses without documento soporte = COP 350K in unnecessary tax. Plus lost IVA credits.
