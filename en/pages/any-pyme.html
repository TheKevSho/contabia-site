Bank reconciliation: Every bank account and credit card reconciled against your accounting system. Auto-matching by amount and date, unmatched items flagged with suggested categories.

Monthly workbook: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow summary, tax summary, bank reconciliation, deductibility report, and quincenal cash flow forecast. Delivered in xlsx, pdf, and pptx.

Anomaly detection: Duplicate charges, vendor cost increases, unrecognized transactions, GMF discrepancies. The agent watches and alerts — you don't have to remember to check.

This applies to every Colombian business

You pay for software. You pay for ads. You pay for cloud services. None of it is deductible without documento soporte.

DIAN Concepto 192/2025 is clear: bank statements and payment receipts from foreign platforms are not valid fiscal support. If you pay for Google Workspace, run Meta ads, subscribe to Canva, host on AWS, or buy from Amazon — you need a documento soporte for each one. Most businesses don't generate them. Most accountants don't either.

Google Workspace (IE) Google Ads (IE) Meta Ads (IE) Amazon (US) Apple (IE) Adobe (US) Canva (AU) AWS (US) Zoom (US) Slack (US) Notion (US) Netflix (US) Spotify (SE) Mailchimp (US) Cloudflare (US) Shopify (CA) Stripe (US) ChatGPT / OpenAI (US)

The math: A business paying COP 2M/month in foreign services (common for any professional services firm, marketing agency, or tech company) loses COP 8.4M/year in unnecessary tax at the 35% corporate rate. Plus IVA credits left on the table. Your agent generates the documento soporte for every one of these, automatically, every month.
