Revenue reconciliation

Five booking sources. Five different payout models. One agent that reconciles them all.

Tour operators sell through OTAs, their own website, WhatsApp inquiries, hotel partnerships, and walk-ups at the dock. Each source has a different commission structure, payout timing, and cancellation policy. Your bookkeeper tracks this in spreadsheets. Your agent does it automatically.

OTA bookings (GetYourGuide, Viator, Airbnb Experiences): Net payouts after platform commission — same gross-up logic as hotel OTAs. The agent records gross revenue and commission separately for accurate channel profitability.

Direct bookings (website, WhatsApp): Full payment received, no commission. The agent tracks these separately so you can see your true direct vs. OTA revenue split and the real cost of each channel.

Hotel partnerships & referrals: Commission-based or net-rate arrangements with referring properties. Each partner may have different terms — the agent tracks per-partner and reconciles against what you actually receive.

Walk-ups & cash sales: Cash and card payments at the point of sale. The agent reconciles POS and cash records against bank deposits, catching timing differences and deposit discrepancies.

Advance deposits & cancellations: Deposits received for future tours are liabilities until the tour happens. Cancellations require refund tracking or credit issuance. The agent manages the advance → revenue recognition → refund lifecycle.

Multi-currency payments: International tourists pay in USD, EUR, or via foreign cards. TRM conversion on the day of the transaction, reconciled against what the bank actually deposits in COP.

Crew & operations

Captains, guides, drivers. Some on payroll, some on contract. All tracked.

Tour operators have a mix of full-time employees, per-trip contractors, and seasonal crew. Payroll obligations differ for each type. The agent tracks who's on nómina (with full prestaciones) and who's on contract (with retefuente on services), and doesn't confuse the two.

FULL-TIME CREW

Per-employee prestaciones tracking — cesantías, prima, vacaciones, intereses. Liquidación worksheet ready when someone departs. Calendar alerts for prima (Jun/Dec), cesantías deposit (Feb), dotación.

PER-TRIP CONTRACTORS

Service contracts with retefuente withholding (4% or 6% depending on declarante status). The agent applies the correct rate, tracks the liability, and doesn't mix contractors into the prestaciones ledger.

SEASONAL SCALING

High season means more crew, more trips, more cash moving. Low season means fixed costs on reduced revenue. The agent forecasts both and alerts you when cash gets tight — before it happens.

Operating cost intelligence

Fuel, maintenance, permits, insurance — tracked per vessel, per vehicle, per asset.

Fuel tracking: Fuel is often your largest variable cost. The agent categorizes fuel receipts by vessel/vehicle, tracks cost per trip, and flags when fuel spend exceeds historical patterns.

Maintenance & repairs: Categorized by asset. Routine maintenance expensed, major repairs capitalized and depreciated. The agent knows the difference and asks when it's ambiguous.

Permits, insurance & renewals: Annual costs amortized monthly so your P&L reflects the true cost per month, not a spike when the bill arrives. The agent alerts you before renewal deadlines.

Equipment depreciation: Boats, vehicles, safety equipment — depreciated on the schedule your accountant sets. The agent posts the monthly entry automatically.

Tax recovery

Your booking platform commissions aren't deductible without documento soporte.

GetYourGuide (Switzerland), Viator/TripAdvisor (Ireland), Airbnb Experiences (Ireland) — all foreign entities. Every commission they keep requires a documento soporte for tax deductibility. Plus your software subscriptions, digital ads, and equipment purchased internationally.

GetYourGuide (CH) Viator (IE) Airbnb Experiences (IE) Booking.com (NL) Google Ads (IE) Meta Ads (IE) TripAdvisor (IE) Amazon (US) Apple (IE) Google Workspace (IE) Canva (AU)

Your agent generates documento soporte for every foreign purchase automatically. At 35% tax rate, recovered deductions often exceed the cost of the service.

Seasonal intelligence

December is not March. Your books should know that.

Tour operators live and die by seasonality. High season cash floods in — but so do crew costs, fuel, and maintenance. Low season revenue drops but fixed costs don't. The quincenal cash flow forecast shows you what's coming: when the prima payments hit, when insurance renews, when the dock fees are due, and whether your cash position survives temporada baja.

With backfill data, the agent knows your seasonal patterns from prior years. Without it, it learns your cycle in real time and gets smarter every month.
